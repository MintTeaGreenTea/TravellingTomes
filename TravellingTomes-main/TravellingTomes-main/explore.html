Here is our selected list of places around Leuven to meet and share your books!

Swartehond

Besides coffee or a desert, you can also choose from a selection of other hot and cold drinks or get a bagel. It’s in a quiet location, placed on a little square with a view of the church. Perfect for warm and rainless days.

Spore Nursery

A hidden gem close to Monseigneur Ladeuzeplein, this inconspicuous establishment is sure to impress you once you enter. There are not many tables to sit at, but if you’re lucky, you’ll get one and can spend some time in this green oasis. The menu is not long but coffee tastes great. Ideal for meeting with someone for a little chat. P. S.: You probably won’t come across many places that would be more photogenic!

Noir

It’s probably the most popular coffee shop in Leuven, famous among both locals and foreigners. Dark drinks but also dark interior, large place that usually doesn’t get too loud. It seems to be a great fit for anything – meeting people, studying or working, reading on the couch by the large window in the back corner. This place offers a large selection of coffee, tea and cold drinks. (and they all taste great!).

Poescafé

This place has a great selection of cakes (vegan ones too) and they taste delicious. The coffee is not greta, being made with an automatic coffee machine, but the presence of cats makes it all worth it!

MOK Specialty Coffee Roastery & Bar

It’s a stylish and modern coffeeshop. It’s one of those places where you can just chill the whole afternoon. Careful : the coffee was strong and bitter, which could not be for everyone's taste. In addition to getting great coffee, you can also pick from selection of teas and few cold, buy a bag of coffee beans from MOK’s roastery or get some pastry to have with your drink. The layout of the coffeeshop is fun and unconventional. It appears that there are just several places at the high table by the window in the front from the outside but once you enter, you realise that the bar does not divide the space into customer- and staff-dedicated areas but is open and part of one integrated area.

Koffie en Staal

Busy, busy, busy. This place is always full of people, everybody engaged in conversations with their friends, partners and families, the staff in the back of the room quickly moving around the bar and coffee machine. The taste of the coffee? Amazing! In addition to having a desert here, you can also get breakfast or lunch. This is a perfect spot for meeting with other people or making a little coffee or lunch break with yourself during a busy day!

Onan

Onan is also a roastery and distributor, so after having your coffee or tea, you can also get a bag of either from their own brand. An the bar, you can order something from large selection of hot and cold coffee drinks or something else (you know the drill, it’s a coffee shop, so there are some hot chocolates, lemonades, or even sparkling water, if you’re feeling adventurous). For espresso-based drinks, you can pick from two kinds of beans, one always being stronger and richer in taste. Because this place can be very loud, with people coming and leaving constantly and the bar not being too separated from the sitting area, resulting in a lot of noise, it is not exactly ideal for studying or working.

KUp Corner

If you are a KU Leuven student, you have probably noticed that there is a little coffee corner inside of Universiteitshal on Naamsestraat. KUp Corner is also operated by Onan but since it is a part of the university hall, it has much calmer and quieter atmosphere. You can come here to study or work, on your own or with your friends, and easily spend the whole day.

Nina Specialty Bar & Shop

There is a large selection of coffee drinks and if you are a fan of strong coffee with intense flavour, you should definitely stop by. The interior is furnished in a funky way that gives a bit of 60s and milkshake bar vibes. The tables are quite small so this place seems ideal for meeting your friends or reading a book. On top of having your coffee and a cake, you can also visit the shop that is part of the establishment and get a bag of coffee, tea or dishes and appliances for preparation of these hot drinks.

Standaard Boekhandel

One of the main bookstores chain in Belgium, and luckily, Leuven has two different spots! The biggest one, in Rector de Somerplein, has one of the best book selections in the city. Despite having a huge English section, this is my go to place when I want to buy books of other languages, like French! One of the best things about this place? Being a chain can provide you with titles not available in store in a short time! Definitely a go to place if you’re passing by on the way to get a Pinocchio Waffle or waiting for the bus. The other store is located in Diestsestraat. My favourite thing about the place is the bookish merchandise (Jane Austen tarots, I’m coming for you!). The place, however, is quite small, especially when it comes to the multilingual section.

Oxfam

Just behind the Oude Markt, Oxfam is a must for the thrifting fans who love to buy second hand. You will find everything! There’s a language section (mainly French, English and Dutch, but also Italian, German and Spanish), a music section where you can find some sheets if you fancy playing, and many more! The only problem it presents is that, being a second hand shop, there are way more Dutch titles than the English ones!

Plato

Plato must be my all time favourite bookstore. A small hidden gem (maybe not so hidden: it’s right next to Ladeuzeplein!) where you can find many books for 25% off. They always have various academic material, plus some other popular titles of the moment, together with several movies and music albums! The owner is a picturesque Belgian guy: a head full of white hair, and he still handwrites the price on each of his books. He doesn’t speak a word of English (or at least never did when speaking to me), but a couple of “goedemiddag” and “dank u” is all that you’ll need!

Acco

If you are a student in Leuven, it’s impossible that you have never heard about Acco: it is THE hotspot for books and stationery you’ll need in class. Going there on an unfortunate day might mean you’ll need to wait to get in - and no, that is not a joke. However, being so well furnished with school books, it may lack in other sections.

Fnac

Fnac has the best English section so far. It has many books, from the most recently published ones to the classics, available in different languages. An obstacle you’ll perhaps face is that, this is not a proper bookstore but rather a multi floor shop, where you can find items not at all book-related. Quite practical I would say, but definitely not matching the it-girl aesthetic!
